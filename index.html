Why This Exists

I built this for my mom.

She’s 82. Her hearing has been fading for years. Conversations became a cycle of “What?” and “Never mind.” — the kind of slow, quiet loss that doesn’t show up in headlines but changes everything about a person’s day.

Hearing aids cost $2,000 to $8,000. She couldn’t afford them. Even the ones she tried didn’t work well — they made everything louder, including the noise. The TV. The traffic. The parts she didn’t need.

What if, instead of making everything louder, we could move speech into the frequencies she can still hear?

That’s VoiceLink. It uses pitch-shifting technology to take the frequencies of speech that have become hard to hear and shift them into ranges that still work. Combined with live captions for anything that slips through. Plus tinnitus relief and smart listening modes that adapt to your environment.

My mom was the first user. Then I realized something — when I sat next to someone with the TV blasting, I couldn’t hear either. VoiceLink isn’t just for people with hearing loss. It’s for everyone around them too.
